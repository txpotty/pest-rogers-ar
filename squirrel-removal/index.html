Recognizing the Signs of a Squirrel Infestation and Their Impact

Understanding the subtle and not-so-subtle signs of a squirrel problem is the first line of defense in protecting your property. Ignoring these indicators can lead to escalating damage and increased costs. Prompt action is key to mitigating the negative consequences associated with a squirrel infestation. While squirrels can be charming to observe in their natural habitat, their presence within a residential or commercial structure is a clear indicator of a problem that requires professional attention.

Auditory Clues: Sounds in Your Walls or Attic

One of the most common indicators of squirrels on your property is the sounds they make. Listen for scratching, scurrying, or gnawing sounds, particularly during the day, as squirrels are primarily diurnal. These sounds are often heard in attics, walls, or chimneys, indicating that squirrels are actively moving and potentially nesting within these spaces.

The sounds can range from light scratching to more pronounced chewing, especially when they are working on expanding entry points or accessing materials for nesting. These noises can be unnerving and disruptive, particularly at night when your home is quiet. The presence of these sounds is a strong signal that an assessment for squirrel removal in Rogers, AR, is warranted.

Visual Evidence: Droppings and Urine Stains

Squirrel droppings are another clear sign of an infestation. These are typically small, pellet-shaped, and can be found in areas where squirrels are active, such as in attics, garages, or near access points. The presence of droppings not only confirms the presence of squirrels but also poses potential health risks due to bacteria and pathogens. Furthermore, squirrel urine can leave behind strong odors and visible stains, indicating areas of frequent activity. These biological contaminants can negatively impact air quality within your home and may require professional cleaning and sanitation.

Identifying areas with droppings and urine helps pest control professionals pinpoint key areas of activity and understand the scale of the infestation. Proper cleanup and disinfection of these areas are essential for restoring a healthy living environment after squirrel removal.

Property Damage: Chewing and Gnawing

Squirrels possess strong teeth that constantly grow, necessitating a need to gnaw on hard surfaces to keep them trimmed. This natural behavior becomes problematic when they choose to gnaw on parts of your home. Common targets include wood trim, siding, electrical wires, and insulation. Damage to electrical wiring is particularly concerning as it can create a significant fire hazard. Gnawing on insulation reduces its effectiveness, leading to increased energy costs.

Evidence of chewing can be found around entry points, within attics, or on exterior structures. This damage can compromise the structural integrity of your home and require costly repairs if not addressed promptly. If you notice unexplained damage to wood or other materials on your property, it is wise to consider the possibility of squirrel activity.

Nest Building Activity

Squirrels build nests, also known as dreys, using a variety of materials like leaves, twigs, and insulation. Finding a drey within your attic, walls, or chimney is a definitive sign of a squirrel infestation. These nests are often constructed for shelter and raising young. The presence of a nest with young squirrels requires specific handling to ensure their safety and the humane removal of the family unit.

Identifying nesting sites helps in planning the removal strategy, ensuring that all squirrels are safely removed from the property. Tampering with or removing a nest without professional guidance can be dangerous and may lead to orphaned young, which can cause further complications.

Exterior Observations: Damaged Bird Feeders or Gardens

Squirrels are well-known for their ability to access bird feeders and dig up gardens in search of food. If you notice your bird feeders are consistently emptied or damaged, or if your garden beds show signs of digging and disturbed plants, squirrels are likely the culprits. While not directly indicative of an interior infestation, these exterior activities suggest a nearby squirrel population that could potentially seek shelter within your home.

Their persistent attempts to access food sources around your property are often the initial sign that squirrels are present in your immediate vicinity. Addressing outdoor attractants can sometimes help deter squirrels from exploring indoor options, but professional intervention is still the most effective way to secure your home.

Discovery of Dead Squirrels

Occasionally, a squirrel might die within a wall cavity, attic, or other inaccessible area of your property. The presence of a foul odor and flies can indicate the location of a deceased animal. This situation requires prompt removal and disinfection due to the potential health risks and unpleasant smell. Dealing with deceased wildlife should always be done with caution and appropriate protective gear.

A decaying animal can attract other pests and create an unsanitary environment. Professional removal services include the safe and complete removal of the carcass and thorough cleaning of the affected area to eliminate odors and potential health hazards.

Unusual Pet Behavior

Your pets, particularly dogs, might react to the presence of squirrels within your home. They may become agitated, bark excessively at walls or ceilings where squirrels are present, or show increased interest in specific areas of the house. Pets' heightened senses can often detect the sounds and smells of pests before humans do.

Paying attention to your pets' behavior can provide an early warning sign of a squirrel infestation. If your dog is persistently focused on a particular area of the house, it's worth investigating further to rule out the presence of wildlife or other pests like rodents or even ants.

Visible Squirrels Entering or Exiting Your Property

Perhaps the most obvious sign of a squirrel problem is actually seeing them enter or exit holes in your roof, siding, or other parts of your house. Observe your property from a distance to see if you notice squirrels frequently coming and going from specific points. This visual confirmation strongly indicates that they have established a home within your structure.

Witnessing this activity clarifies the presence of an infestation and highlights the specific entry points that need to be sealed during the exclusion process. Identifying these pathways is crucial for a successful squirrel removal plan.

Damage to Exterior Structures

Beyond the interior, squirrels can also cause damage to exterior structures as they attempt to gain access. This can include chewed fascia boards, damaged vents, or holes in screen doors and windows. Any area of your home's exterior that provides an entry point or a surface for gnawing is vulnerable to squirrel activity.

Regularly inspecting the exterior of your home for signs of damage is a proactive measure in detecting potential squirrel problems early. Addressing minor damage promptly can prevent squirrels from exploiting these weaknesses and gaining entry into your home.

Increased Activity During Certain Times of Day

Squirrels are most active during the early morning and late afternoon hours. Observing an increase in squirrel activity around your home during these times, particularly near potential entry points, can be a strong indicator of an existing or developing problem. Their foraging and movement patterns are typically concentrated during these periods.

Noting the times you observe squirrels and where they are focused can provide valuable information to pest control professionals when developing a removal strategy.

Addressing Other Potential Pests

It's worth noting that some of the signs of squirrel infestation can overlap with those of other common pests in the Rogers area, such as raccoons, mice, or even birds.
